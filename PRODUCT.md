# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary user is the owner of the app: someone opening the web page while drinking tea, using it as a quiet companion and atmospheric window rather than as a task-heavy tool.

The use context is slow, domestic, and ambient. The interface may stay open for a long time while the user is physically present with real tea, so the product should support looking, listening, and settling into a mood without demanding attention.

## Product Purpose

Tea Companion creates a calm web-based tea companion scene with selectable ambience, weather, perspective, and seating context. Its purpose is to make a screen feel like a quiet window beside a tea table: scenery in the window, tea objects in the foreground, and controls that disappear when observation matters.

Success means the user wants to leave it open while drinking tea, can quickly adjust the mood when needed, and can enter an immersive mode where the interface steps aside.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, restrained, immersive.

The tone should feel intentional and meditative, with craft in the composition rather than decoration for its own sake. It should respect the physical ritual of tea and avoid loud gamified feedback.

## Anti-references

- Do not look like a direct filter applied to a reference photo.
- Do not look like a generic admin dashboard or settings panel.
- Do not look like a low-poly game demo unless intentionally scoped as a separate visual mode.
- Do not rely on AI-looking decorative clutter, generic glass panels, or noisy ornamental effects.

## Design Principles

- Make the scene the product: controls should support the atmosphere, then get out of the way.
- Preserve the spatial idea of scenery inside the window and tea objects in front of the window.
- Favor quiet composition over feature density; every visible element should earn its place.
- Create original scene language from references rather than copying or filtering source images.
- Keep interaction reversible and low-pressure, especially around immersive observation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Support reduced motion preferences, maintain readable text contrast, and keep the immersive mode easy to exit. The app should remain usable from keyboard and pointer interactions, with controls large enough for relaxed use.
